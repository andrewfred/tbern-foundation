Fact Of The Day

Did You Know? One-Third of the Earth's Stored Carbon Is Found in Arctic Tundra Soil

The arctic tundra is characterized by extremely cold temperatures and land that remains frozen year-round. This frozen soil or permafrost plays an important role in the cycle of nutrients such as carbon. As temperatures rise globally, this frozen ground melts and releases stored carbon from the soil into the atmosphere. The release of carbon could impact global climate change by increasing temperatures.
